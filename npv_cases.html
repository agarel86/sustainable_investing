NPV Exercises: ESG and Investment Decisions

Here you can find NPV exercises in which the ESG-friendly project starts with the lower baseline NPV. You must assess whether the conclusion changes once an ESG-related factor affects one key NPV channel: cash flows, cost of capital, demand from customers, or initial investment.

Each case includes a short-horizon and long-horizon view to highlight how short-term decision rules can ignore long-term damages, regulation, technology shifts, financing constraints, and stakeholder impacts.

Channel: Cash flows Channel: Discount rate / cost of capital Channel: Customer demand / declining revenues Channel: Initial investment

Case logic

In every exercise, the conventional project looks better at first. The ESG-friendly project or ESG-compliant version initially appears to destroy value or to offer a lower NPV. The question is whether the conclusion changes once sustainability-related effects are allowed to affect the NPV.

Four channels used in the generator

ChannelHow it enters the NPVTypical ESG trigger
Cash flowsOperating costs or net annual cash flow changeCarbon prices, fines, worker injuries, supply disruptions, remediation costs
Discount rateHigher required return lowers present valueTransition risk, legal risk, financing penalties, lender restrictions
DemandRevenues decline over timeCustomer switching, regulation, greener substitutes, reputation loss
Initial investmentHigher up-front capital needed reduces NPV immediatelyInvestor shortages, bank exclusion policies, costly bond issuance, financing frictions

Calculation notes: level cash flows use the annuity factor AF = (1 - (1 + r)^(-n)) / r. Declining cash flows use the growing annuity formula PV = CF₁ × [1 - ((1 + g)/(1 + r))^n] / (r - g), where g can be negative.
